What is Maintainance Calories?

Maintainance claories are the amount of calories you need to consume in a day to maintain how you are right now. Let suppose your weight today is 65 kilograms and now you neither want your weight to be below 65 nor above 65. So to maintain this you need to consume calories in such a way that the weight is maintained. This amount of calories you need to maintain your weight are called Maintainance claories.


How to know how much is your Maintainance Calories?

This is might sound tricky or hard but no it's easy. Before telling you about how to know your maintainence calories, there are some things to be taken under consideration. There are sites which tell you to enter your current height and weight so that they could tell your maintainence calories but they aren't trustworthy. For example caloriecounter is a site which does the above mentioned things, I once tried this and it told me to eat around 7000 calories a day to maintain my weight. 7000 is nearly equal to 1 kilogram so when you eat 7000 calories/day you gain 1kg weight/day. So it's more problem, my opinion is not to try them out.
The actual way this- Take a measurement of your weight on day 1, let the weight be 70. Now eat home made food or the one you like, weigh your weight on day 2. If your weight increased to 70.3 to 4 grams, this means you ate a lot than your maintainence calories. This was a one day observation, so observe it for a week. If your weight gradually increased then you need to reduce your intake. If your weight gradually decreases then you need to increase a little bit of your food intake. After these acts, your weight once get undisturbed for a week then that's your maintainence calories and you also know how much you need to reduce your food intake for weight loss and how much you need to increase your food intake for weight gain.
Yes you might seem thi a heck of a process and get demotivated right now but know that Fitness in not a destination, its a journey till the end. So you gotta work these things for the whole life itself.
